# Session Topic Bank

A running list of future session ideas, organized by audience level. Each entry is a planned topic, not a delivered session — move entries to `/sessions` once actually presented.

---

## Beginner / General Audience

### "How People Get Hacked" (Delivered — 2026-07-19)
See [`/sessions/how-people-get-hacked.md`](../sessions/how-people-get-hacked.md)

### "Your Digital Footprint Is Bigger Than You Think"
A hands-on session where attendees search their own name/handles live and see what a stranger could find. High engagement potential since it's participatory rather than lecture-style.

### "Password Hygiene Isn't Boring, It's Just Explained Badly"
Practical session on password managers, passphrase construction, and why "P@ssw0rd123!" isn't actually clever.

### "The Anatomy of a Scam Message"
Live breakdown of real (redacted) phishing/scam messages — SMS, email, WhatsApp — teaching pattern recognition rather than a static list of red flags.

## Intermediate / Technical-Curious Audience

### "OSINT 101: Finding Things the Legal Way"
Introduces core OSINT methodology (from the recon checklist writeup) with a live, sanitized demonstration.

### "MFA Isn't Magic: Understanding Session Hijacking"
Deeper technical dive into AiTM attacks — builds directly on the Medium phishing article, good for a more technical crowd.

### "Reading a Phishing Kit Like a Researcher"
Walks through the structure of Phishing-as-a-Service kits at a conceptual level (no operational/replication detail) — pairs well with the published Medium piece.

## Advanced / Peer Audience (Other Researchers)

### "Threat Actor Profiling: How Public Attribution Actually Works"
Uses the threat-actor repo entries (APT28, Lazarus, LockBit, etc.) as case studies in how public attribution research is actually built from open sources.

### "Bug Bounty Methodology: Recon to Report"
Joint session with Bassel — his bug bounty process end to end, positioned as the "sister session" to the OSINT-focused talks.

### "Building a Threat Intel Habit: Notes, Sources, and Staying Current"
Meta-session about the actual research workflow behind the writeups — how to build and maintain a personal threat intel practice.

---

## Notes
- Beginner sessions build audience size and reach; advanced sessions build peer credibility — worth alternating between the two categories rather than only doing one type
- Every session here should ideally map to an existing or planned Medium article, so prep work is never wasted on a single format
