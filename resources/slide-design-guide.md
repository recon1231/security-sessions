# Slide & Visual Design Guide

Consistency guidelines for session slides — keeps every talk looking like part of the same brand instead of one-off decks.

---

## Structure
- One idea per slide — if you need two sentences to explain a slide's title, split it into two slides
- Big, minimal text — slides support what you're saying, they don't replace it
- Every technical section gets at least one visual (diagram, screenshot, or simple illustration) — pure text walls lose a live audience fast

## Visual Style
- Dark background, high-contrast text — reads better in most room lighting than light backgrounds
- One accent color used consistently across all slides (ties into your personal brand colors from your social profiles)
- Real screenshots only when fully redacted — blur/box out anything sensitive before it goes on a slide, every time, no exceptions

## Opening Slide
- Title, your name, and one line describing the session — no lengthy bio slide, save that for verbal introduction if needed

## Closing Slide
- Clear call-to-action: follow links, Medium article link, or "ask me anything" — never end on a blank "Thank You" slide with no next step

## Redaction Checklist (run before every session, every time)
- [ ] Any real email addresses blurred/removed
- [ ] Any real IP addresses, domains, or usernames tied to real people removed or replaced with placeholders
- [ ] Any client/company names removed unless explicitly cleared for public use
- [ ] Screenshots checked for accidental sensitive info in browser tabs, notifications, or background windows
