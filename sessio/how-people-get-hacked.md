# How People Get Hacked

**Date:** 2026-07-19
**Audience:** General / Beginner
**Format:** Live talk

## Overview
A beginner-friendly session breaking down the most common real-world ways people get compromised — reframing "hacking" away from Hollywood-style technical breaches and toward social engineering, credential exposure, and public information gathering.

## Session Outline
1. Opening — You Don't Get Hacked, You Get Social Engineered
2. Phishing — The #1 Entry Point
3. The MFA Myth (session hijacking / AiTM attacks)
4. Weak & Reused Passwords
5. Public Wi-Fi & Fake Networks
6. OSINT — What Attackers Learn About You Before They Even Attack
7. Malware Delivery Beyond "Don't Click Suspicious Links"
8. Closing — What Actually Protects You

## Key Points Covered
- Modern phishing kits (Phishing-as-a-Service) have industrialized credential theft
- Adversary-in-the-Middle (AiTM) attacks steal active sessions, not just passwords — meaning traditional MFA alone is no longer fully sufficient
- OSINT techniques show how much attacker reconnaissance happens before any technical attack even begins
- Practical, low-effort defenses (password managers, hardware security keys, skepticism toward urgency) matter more than most people assume

## Audience Engagement
Good engagement overall — the OSINT section (showing how much public info is discoverable) generated the most reaction, confirming it's a strong differentiator to lean into for future sessions.
